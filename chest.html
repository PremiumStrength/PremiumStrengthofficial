BEST CHEST WORKOUT
Develop a sculpted upper body by focusing on strengthening your chest muscles effectively

To Target Upper Chest Muscle

Incline Dumbel Fly

The incline dumbbell fly is a strength training exercise that targets upper chest muscles, particularly the pectoralis major, and also engages anterior deltoids, triceps, and core muscles.

Incline Dumbell Press

The Incline Dumbbell Press is a variation of the standard incline barbell bench press, targeting upper chest muscles, shoulders, and triceps, requiring a 30-45 degrees angle incline bench.

Low to High Cable Fly

Low to high cable flys are a chest fly exercise that strengthens pushing muscles like chest, biceps, triceps, and shoulders, mimicking the pull of the clavicular pectoralis.

To Target Middle Chest Muscle

Bench Press

Bench press is a weightlifting exercise targeting the chest, shoulders, and triceps, working the pectoralis major and minor, anterior deltoids, and triceps brachii muscles, while also working the serratus anterior and latissimus dorsi.

Peck Deck Machine

A pec deck machine is a simple, effective workout that builds chest muscles, strengthens shoulders and core, and can be adjusted based on fitness level.

Mid Cable Fly

The cable chest fly targets pectoralis major and minor chest muscles, with the pec major responsible for arm adduction, inward rotation, and elevation.

To Target Lower Chest Muscle

Chest Dips

The chest dip targets chest muscles, triceps, rhomboids, neck, latissimus dorsi, and trapezius muscles, engaging anterior deltoids, arms, rhomboids, levator scapulae, and back.

Decline Barbell Press

The decline bench press targets lower pectoral muscles, triceps, deltoids, and serratus anterior, reducing shoulder stress and focusing more on the lower chest compared to flat and incline bench presses.

High to Low Cable Fly

High to low cable flys are chest muscle exercises using adjustable pulleys on a cable machine, aiming to strengthen and tone pectoralis major and minor muscles, requiring proper form.
